Project - Test a Perceptual Phenomenon¶

Statistics: The Science of Decisions ProjectInstructions Background Information In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the inkin which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent wordscondition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent wordscondition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

2. What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.
In [ ]:
The null hypothesis states the incongruent words condition has no effect upon
the population's time to complete the task:

H: congruent = incongruent

congruent being the population mean for the congruent condition and
incongruent being the population mean for the incongruent condition.

The alternative hypothesis states the incongruent words condition
increases the time it takes for a participant to complete the task.

Ha: congruent <= incongruent

As we do not know the populations' standard deviation and the sample size is
less than 30, a paired or dependent-samples, one-tailed t-test is required in
the positive direction as every participant participates in both conditions
and we expect the incongruent words condition to increase the time a
participant takes to complete the task.
