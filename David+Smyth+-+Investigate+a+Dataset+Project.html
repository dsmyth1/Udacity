Utilizing the The Lahman Baseball Database of Major League Baseball statistics from 1871 through 2016, this data analysis seeks to determine the effects of the Live-ball and Steroid eras on player offensive performance.

American professional baseball can be divided into three eras, the Dead-ball era, the Live-ball era and the Steroid era. During the Dead-ball era, which ended in 1920, pitchers were favored by the re-utilization of a single baseball throughout the game. This era was followed by the Live-ball era in which baseballs were replaced durimg the game at the first sign of wear resulting in increased offensive performance. The steroid era also saw increased offensive production.

Statistically, what could be said to be the effect of these subsequent eras upon player offensive performance? Furthermore, given the said enhancement of player offensive performance, what player, in retrospect, could be said to be the true home run king.

Sharing Findings¶

Computing the standard score or z-score for the steroid era's home run record shows the record, 73 home runs, is 8.79 standard deviations away from the mean for that era. Were this the standard deviation for the maximum home runs hit during the dead-ball era, the maximum home runs would have been 18.75. Instead, the maximum home runs hit during the dead-ball era was 29. Likewise, the standard deivations of 29 home runs during the dead-ball era results in a standard or z-score of 13.83. This would translate to 112.63 home runs hit during the steroid era which is 39.63 home runs more than the record! Thus controlling for ball liveliness and performance enhancement, the all-time professional baseball home run record can be said to be Babe Ruth's 29 homeruns in 1919 for the Boston Redsox. It is a greater statistical achievement than Roger Maris's 61 home runs in 1961 (live ball era) or Barry Bond's 73 home runs in 2001 (steroid era). These findings are tentative and only grossly control for era as the independent variable in the study. Further refinement of variables such as the foul-strike rule or ballpark dimensions could serve to indicate more exactly the factors by which the Live-ball and Steroid eras were most effective in increasing offensive performance.
